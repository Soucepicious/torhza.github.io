Folklure Magazine

Brief /Description:

—For "Folklure," the goal was to create a magazine that would introduce the rich and diverse Amazigh culture to a wider audience. This project was more than just an academic exercise; it was a mission to preserve and celebrate the heritage of the Amazigh people, ensuring their stories, art, and traditions were shared and appreciated.

Client

University Project
/Integrated Print Media Design

Project

Culture Magazine

Role

Editorial Design

Solution/Outcome:

I developed a visually engaging magazine that featured stories, poetry, artwork, music, and traditional recipes, all centered around Amazigh culture. The design was thoughtfully crafted to not only highlight the beauty of this heritage but also to connect with readers on a deeper level. The outcome was a publication that successfully captured the essence of Amazigh culture, helping to keep it alive and relevant for future generations.

Reflection/Learnings:

—Through this project, I learned the importance of cultural preservation through design. I discovered how design can be a powerful tool for storytelling and community building. I also gained valuable insights into balancing tradition with modern aesthetics, ensuring that the magazine was both respectful of its roots and engaging to a contemporary audience.
